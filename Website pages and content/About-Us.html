This business was founded by Fungai Makombe, my father’s younger brother Paul Makombe. In 2014 this business was
officially registered as a trade and export business. This business used to import and export goods only no manufacturing
was being done as Fungai Makombe conducted this business using his phone to communicate orders
to those he worked with so they receive the goods and are paid for packaging the goods,
then a buyer is found, and he would get paid from the comfort of his home.
There were times when he needed to be there before goods (which were peanuts and Kapenta fish)
were transported or traveled to certain parts of the world in African countries.
This was when the quality of the goods needed to be checked
, and he had to make sure that everything was according to the taste of the specific client.

Makombe, FT.2023.Images of Kapenta fish, and Peanuts

Fast forward to 2 to 3 years, this was when he started hiring workers and was a more hands-on businessperson. He has said that, and I quote
“I’m doing this for the family that surrounds me that does not have a stable source of income, so it helps them “.
He could have easily continued doing business on his phone. This meant that now instead of just
paying his professional partners he hired family and those around him that he saw needed
help to start parking the Kapenta fish in packaging that were then parked in packs
and sold in balks, the peanuts come in raw form, and needed to be pealed and delivered
to those that manufacture peanut butter, and he hired workers to do that too,
and my siblings and I would sometimes help to get extra pocket money.

Then everything went downhill when COVID-19 struck, and it was no longer safe so every country
went under lockdown meaning no income was being generated but the government created means to
make sure every household was fed.When the lockdown was lifted it was safer to work and go back to school.
My father Paul Makombe had lost the means of creating income as his local business had lost goods,
he decided to work with his younger brother in his business as it had much more potential.
That was when Fungai Makombe’s business started to manufacture tertiary goods
(which is the final product produced from raw materials) in his case he started producing
peanut butter in the year 2020 right after the lockdown was lifted. This was when he now had
to find a more suitable place to work, and the machinery used for manufacturing peanut butter.
This business still to this day has no social media platform to advertise its Goods and Services.
